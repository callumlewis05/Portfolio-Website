Objective

The primary objective of blackjack is to beat the dealer's hand without exceeding a total of 21 points.

Card Values

- Number cards (2-10) are worth their face value.
- Face cards (Jack, Queen, King) are each worth 10 points.
- Aces can be worth either 1 point or 11 points, depending on which value benefits the player's hand more.

Betting

Before the cards are dealt, each player must place their bet. Players put their chips in the betting area on the blackjack table. The minimum and maximum bet limits depend on the table being played at.

Gameplay

1. Each player and the dealer are initially dealt two cards. The dealer deals one card face-up (the "upcard") and one card face-down (the "hole card").
2. Players take turns deciding how to play their hands. They can choose from the following options:

- Hit: Take another card to increase the total value of the hand. Players can continue to hit as long as they don't exceed 21 points. If they exceed 21, they bust and lose the hand.
- Stand: Keep the current total and end their turn.
- Double Down: Double their original bet and receive one more card. After receiving the additional card, they must stand.
- Split: If the initial two cards are of the same rank (e.g., two 7s or two 8s), they can choose to split the cards into two separate hands, each with a new bet equal to the original bet. They will receive one more card for each hand, and they can play each hand separately.

3. After all players have completed their hands, the dealer reveals their hole card.
4. The dealer hits until they reach a total of 17 or higher. If the dealer busts, all remaining players win. If the dealer doesn't bust, the player with a higher hand total than the dealer's without exceeding 21 wins. Ties result in a push (no win or loss).
5. Winning players are paid equal money to their bets in addition to the original bet, while blackjack (an Ace and a 10-value card) pays 3:2 in addition to the original bet. Players who split should treat each hand separately and refer to the previous statements for the pay-out on each hand, with players who double down receiving the appropriate pay-out for their new bet. Players who go bust or players who have a lower total than the dealer lose their bet. Players whose total is equal to the dealer will push which results in no loss or gain of money.
